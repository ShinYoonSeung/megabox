TADA: A Portrait of Korean Startups

The story of the six-month struggle after the Tada Prohibition Act.
"I have to survive somehow!" Tada, a mobility platform that was called Uber in Korea and grew explosively. While securing 1 million users nine months after its launch, it is embroiled in a legal battle due to opposition from the taxi industry. On the day of acquittal in the first trial amid a heated controversy, all team members gather together to celebrate it as a "paper cup wine party." However, just 14 days later, there is news of a bolt from the blue that the "Tada Prohibition Act" has been passed. Can they break through this worst crisis and revive Tada?

Korea's first documentary film about "Start-up," which will be unveiled to the world with the story of mobility platform Tada.
